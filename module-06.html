Module 06 · Phase Two · Design

Cover Design

Design a cover that sells your book before anyone reads a word. Genre conventions, file specs, and the three-second test.

Module 6 · Lesson Video
Overview

What You'll Accomplish

Readers absolutely judge books by their covers. Your cover is a sales tool — it communicates genre, quality, and promise in under three seconds. A cover that looks self-published without looking professional will cost you readers, reviews, and revenue.

This module teaches you what makes a cover work, how to brief a designer or create one yourself, and how to produce the exact file specifications required by every major publishing platform.

Module 6 Deliverable

A completed book cover in two formats: a digital/eBook cover (JPG or PNG, 2,560 x 1,600 pixels minimum) and a print-ready cover with spine and back cover (PDF, to platform specification).

Lesson Content

What You'll Learn

Lesson 1: The Five Elements of a Professional Cover
1

Hierarchy

Title, author name, and imagery have a clear visual order. The eye knows where to go.

2

Typography

Fonts match the genre. Serif for literary/historical. Sans-serif for thriller/business. Script for romance/memoir.

3

Imagery

The central image communicates genre and tone immediately. High resolution, uncluttered, emotionally resonant.

4

Color

The palette matches the emotional register. Dark and moody for thriller. Warm and soft for romance. Bold and clean for self-help.

5

Thumbnail Readability

Your cover will be seen as a small image on Amazon. If the title is unreadable at thumbnail size, it needs to be redesigned.

Fiction Conventions

  • Thriller: dark palette, bold sans-serif, single dramatic element
  • Romance: warm tones, human figures, script or elegant serif title
  • Fantasy: illustrated or dramatic landscape, ornate typography
  • Literary: abstract, typographic, or photographic and understated

Non-Fiction Conventions

  • Self-help: clean, modern, bold title dominant
  • Memoir: author photo or evocative image, warm palette
  • Business: bold title, minimal design, authoritative typography
  • Health & wellness: bright, clean, aspirational imagery
Lesson 2: Your Cover Design Options
A

Hire a Professional Designer (Recommended)

Budget $200–$800 for a specialist. Find designers on Reedsy, 99designs, or The Book Designer. Look for a portfolio in your specific genre.

B

Use a Template Tool (Budget Option)

Canva, Book Brush, or Reedsy's free cover creator. Start with a template from your exact genre. Use only licensed images.

Lesson 3: Cover Specifications

eBook Cover

  • Format: JPG or PNG
  • Dimensions: 2,560 x 1,600px minimum
  • Resolution: 72 DPI (RGB)
  • File size: Under 50MB

Print Cover (KDP)

  • Format: PDF (no compression)
  • Includes: front + spine + back as one file
  • Bleed: 0.125 inches on all sides
  • Resolution: 300 DPI minimum (CMYK)

Cover Tip

Order a proof copy of your print book before publishing. Colors shift between screen (RGB) and print (CMYK), and the spine width must be exact. A proof copy costs $3–$10 and prevents costly errors from reaching readers.

Assignments

Your Work

Complete each assignment before moving to the next module.

6.1

Cover Research

Find 10 covers from recently published books in your exact genre and sub-genre. Describe what they have in common: typography, color palette, imagery type, layout.

6.2

Your Cover Brief

Write your complete cover brief: (1) Genre and sub-genre. (2) Three comp covers and why. (3) Dominant emotion the cover should convey. (4) Specific imagery or symbols. (5) Exact title, subtitle, author name. (6) Colors or imagery to avoid.

6.3

Designer Research or Tool Selection

If hiring: list 3 designers whose portfolio matches your genre with pricing and turnaround. If using a tool: which tool, which template, and your customization plan.

6.4

The Three-Second Test

Show your cover draft to 5 people for 3 seconds each. Ask: What genre is this? Who is it for? Would you click? Record their responses and any changes the feedback suggests.

6.5

Cover Completion Checklist

Confirm: Title readable at thumbnail. Author name correct. Genre communicated clearly. Image licensed for commercial use. Files exported in correct formats.
